Buddhism Notes

四諦 Four Noble Truths

  1. 苦 Dukkha: Suffering exists in life.
  2. 集 Samudaya: Craving causes suffering.
  3. 滅 Nirodha: Ending craving ends suffering.
  4. 道 Magga: The eightfold path ends craving.

八正道 Eightfold Path

    般若 Prajna: Wisdom

  1. 正見 Right view: True sight, open attitude, understanding.
  2. 正思惟 Right intention: Intention of renunciation and good will.

    3. 尸羅 Sila: Virtue

  3. 正語 Right speech: Do not purposelessly lie, or verbally harm.
  4. 正業 Right action: Do not harm others. Follow the five precepts.
  5. 正命 Right livelihood: Do not take a harmful job. Appreciate your job.

    6. 三昧 Samadhi: Mind

  6. Right effort: Abandon unwholesome states, arouse wholesome states.
  7. 正念 Right mindfulness: Mental true sight. Avoid biased and unclear thinking.
  8. 正定 Right concentration: Concentration; meditation.

四梵住 Brahma-vihara: Sublime attitudes

  1. 慈 Metta: Loving kindness, hope for someone to be well.
  2. 悲 Karuna: Compassion, the hope for someone to not suffer.
  3. 喜 Mudita: Sympathetic joy, rejoicing in others' happiness.
  4. 捨 Upeksha: Equanimity, evenness of mind despite losses and gains.

三法印 Three Marks of Existence

  1. 苦 Dukkha: Dissatisfaction, unease, suffering
  2. 無常 Anicca: Impermanence
  3. 無我 Anatta: Nonself; the "self" is just a name for a collection of parts

五戒 Five Precepts

  1. 不殺生 To refrain from killing or destroying life.
  2. 不偷盜 To refrain from taking what is not offered.
  3. 不邪淫 To refrain from sexual misconduct.
  4. 不妄語 To refrain from lying and gossiping.
  5. 不飲酒 To refrain from intoxicants that cause heedlessness.

四正勤Four Right Exertions

  1. 已生恶令永断，对已生之恶，使永除断。For the abandoning of unskillful states
  2. 未生恶令不生，对未生之恶，使更不生。For the non-arising of unskillful states
  3. 已生善令增长，对已生之善使增长。For the sustaining of skillful states
  4. 未生善令得生，对未生之善使生。For the arising of skillful states

五蘊 Five "Aggregates"

  1. 色 Form
  2. 受 Sensation
  3. 想 Cognition
  4. 行 Mental formation
  5. 識 Consciousness

十界 Ten Worlds

  1. 地獄界 Hell
  2. 餓鬼界 Hunger
  3. 畜生界 Animality
  4. 修羅界 Anger and selfishness
  5. 人界 Tranquility
  6. 天界 Rapture
  7. 聲聞界 Learning
  8. 緣覺界 Realization
  9. 菩薩界 Bodhisattvahood
  10. 佛界 Buddhahood
