Buddhism Notes

四諦 Four Noble Truths

  1. 苦 Dukkha: Suffering exists in life.
  2. 集 Samudaya: Craving causes suffering.
  3. 滅 Nirodha: Ending craving ends suffering.
  4. 道 Magga: The eightfold path ends craving.

八正道 Eightfold Path

    般若 Prajna: Wisdom

  1. 正見 Right view: True sight, open attitude, understanding.
  2. 正思惟 Right intention: Intention of renunciation and good will.

    3. 尸羅 Sila: Virtue

  3. 正語 Right speech: Do not purposelessly lie, or verbally harm.
  4. 正業 Right action: Do not harm others. Follow the five precepts.
  5. 正命 Right livelihood: Do not take a harmful job. Appreciate your job.

    6. 三昧 Samadhi: Mind

  6. Right effort: Abandon unwholesome states, arouse wholesome states.
  7. 正念 Right mindfulness: Mental true sight. Avoid biased and unclear thinking.
  8. 正定 Right concentration: Concentration; meditation.

四梵住 Brahma-vihara: Sublime attitudes

  1. Metta: Loving kindness, hope for someone to be well.
  2. Karuna: Compassion, the hope for someone to not suffer.
  3. Mudita: Sympathetic joy, rejoicing in others' happiness.
  4. Upeksha: Equanimity, evenness of mind despite losses and gains.

三法印 Three Marks of Existence

  1. 苦 Dukkha: Dissatisfaction, unease, suffering
  2. 無常 Anicca: Impermanence
  3. 無我 Anatta: Nonself; the "self" is just a name for a collection of parts

五戒 Five Precepts

  1. 不殺生 To refrain from killing or destroying life.
  2. 不偷盜 To refrain from taking what is not offered.
  3. 不邪淫 To refrain from sexual misconduct.
  4. 不妄語 To refrain from lying and gossiping.
  5. 不飲酒 To refrain from intoxicants that cause heedlessness.

Four Frames of Reference

  1. Contemplation of body
  2. Contemplation of feelings
  3. Contemplation of consciousness
  4. Contemplation of mental qualities

Four Right Exertions

  1. For the non-arising of unskillful states
  2. For the abandoning of unskillful states
  3. For the arising of skillful states
  4. For the sustaining of skillful states

五蘊 Five Aggregates

  1. 色 Form
  2. 受 Sensation
  3. 想 Cognition
  4. 行 Mental formation
  5. 識 Consciousness

七覺支 Seven Factors of Enlightenment

  1. 念覺支 Sati: Mindfulness
  2. 擇法覺 Dhamma Vicaya: Investigation
  3. 精進覺支 Viriya: Energy
  4. 喜覺支 Piti: Joy
  5. 輕安覺支 Passaddhi: Tranquility
  6. 定覺支 Samadhi: Concentration
  7. 舍覺支 Upeksha: Equanimity

五蓋 Five Hindrances

  1. 貪欲蓋 Kamacchanda: Sensual desire
  2. 瞋恚蓋 Byapada: Anger
  3. 睡眠蓋 Thina-middha: Sloth
  4. 掉悔蓋 Uddhacca-kukkacca: Worry
  5. 疑蓋 Vicikiccha: Doubt

十界 Ten Worlds

  1. 地獄界 Hell
  2. 餓鬼界 Hunger
  3. 畜生界 Animality
  4. 修羅界 Anger, selfishness
  5. 人界 Tranquility
  6. 天界 Rapture
  7. 聲聞界 Learning
  8. 緣覺界 Realization
  9. 菩薩界 Bodhisattvahood
  10. 佛界 Buddhahood
